• Our Aiming Target

A complex connection between social experiences and cultural environments influences sexual preferences (kinks and fetishes). As cultural norms and societal expectations play significant roles in shaping these preferences in China and South Korea, understanding these influences can provide insights into the development of sexual behaviors and preferences among university students.

Our project mainly focuses on two research questions:

  1. How do social experiences and cultural environments influence the development of kinks and fetishes of university students in China and South Korea?
  2. What are the three most popular kinks / fetishes among South Koreans and Chinese?

Our project aims to explore the factors that contribute to the development of kinks and fetishes and their psychological impact on sexual relationships. By focusing on university students in China and South Korea, the study seeks to uncover often overlooked cultural and social dynamics.

We are also curious about the sexual differences between South Korea and China and aim to figure out the most popular kinks and fetishes in these two countries, which may also support our discussion

• Our Research Method

Before the survey, we do literature review to identify historical studies and hypotheses related to top three kinks and fetishes worldwide. Then we conduct survey that includes questions related to kinks and fetishes(ex., preference of kinks and fetishes they have, age of when they first found out, etc.) to collect data and insight from diverse South Korean and Chinese students. And there will also be an interview on people who are willing to tell their own personal story on how they have found their fetishes or how it affects their sex life.(ex., Cultural impact, personal experience, etc.) We will Analyze the survey responses and interview data to evaluate the hypotheses and determine the top three kinks and fetishes in South Korea and China while comparing the results with existing statistics to validate the conclusions.

• What are kinks and fetishes

Kink is basically any erotic or sexual interest that deviates from the "normal" or expected sexual path. A kink can be part of a person's overall sexual makeup (i.e., you occasionally like to be spanked, you’re here for a bit of latex), or very much the focus of a person's eroticism (i.e., you want to get spanked every time you have sex). (Carol Queen)

The term comes from the Dutch word "kink," referring to a twist or bend in a rope. And it's now used to describe "things that are of particular sexual curiosity and interest to someone, particularly those things, behaviors, or practices that are outside of normative sexual behavior (Kyle Zrenchik)."

Fetish is typically concentrated on an object or a body part. It usually involves an object or a specific body part (or its function) that is the focus of a person's sexual interest. It’s a NEED that is required in order to reach full sexual satisfaction. A person with a foot fetish, for instance, may want to interact sexually with feet to the exclusion of other things.

Outside of sex, the word "fetish" is also used to describe a type of object that's conferred magical powers, and that's how you can think of it in reference to sex. Some people have objects or body parts that are sexually enticing to them and hold power over them in a way that is out of "the ordinary." (Carol Queen)

• Differences

Generally speaking, fetishes concentrate on objects or body parts, while kinks can be anything that is considered an unusual sexual practice or fantasy.

Also, Kyle Zrenchik pointed out that "Kink is something that one may or may not include in their sexual behavior as a way to enhance pleasure and subjective experience. A fetish is something that primarily defines and is inextricably linked to one's sexual behavior."

Aliyah Moore suggested using three simple questions to define them:

  1. Am I aroused by a thing or an action?
  2. Do I need it to be present to be aroused?
  3. Can I enjoy solo sex without it being present?

"If the answer to item 2 is yes and item 3 no, then you have a fetish. On the other hand, if you answered no and yes for items 2 and 3, respectively, then you have a kink," she says.
